### Reflection

Applying Agile methodology to the Tutor Booking System project presented several challenges, particularly as a solo developer responsible for fulfilling multiple Scrum roles. One of the main difficulties was translating structured system requirements and use cases into user stories while ensuring they adhered to Agile principles such as INVEST. Although the functional requirements were clearly defined, breaking them down into smaller, independent, and testable user stories required careful thought to avoid overlap and redundancy.

Another challenge was prioritization. Deciding which features should be classified as Must-have versus Should-have or Could-have required balancing technical feasibility with user needs. As the only stakeholder, I had to simulate different perspectives, including students, tutors, and administrators. This often created internal conflict, especially when deciding whether to prioritize usability features or technical requirements such as security and performance. For example, while encryption is not directly visible to users, it is critical for system integrity and had to be prioritized accordingly.

Effort estimation was also challenging without real team input. Assigning story points required assumptions about complexity and development time, which may not fully reflect real-world scenarios. Similarly, sprint planning required distributing tasks across different roles such as backend developer, frontend developer, and QA engineer, even though all responsibilities fall on a single individual. This highlighted the importance of role separation in Agile teams and how it contributes to efficiency and accountability.

Another difficulty was aligning Agile practices with previously structured documentation. Earlier assignments followed a more traditional, plan-driven approach, whereas Agile emphasizes flexibility and iterative development. Bridging this gap required maintaining traceability between functional requirements, use cases, and user stories, ensuring consistency across all project artifacts.

Despite these challenges, the process provided valuable insights into Agile development. It emphasized the importance of delivering a Minimum Viable Product (MVP) by focusing on core features such as authentication, search, and booking. It also reinforced the need for continuous prioritization and adaptability. Overall, this assignment improved my understanding of how Agile methodologies can be applied in practice, even within the constraints of an individual project.
